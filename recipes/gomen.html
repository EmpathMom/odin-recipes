Gomen Ethiopian Cuisine

Description

These collards can be served as a vegan main dish or as a hearty side. If possible, serve with injera, the traditional crepe-like bread common in Ethiopia (see Associated Recipe).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs 2 bunches collard greens
  • 2 tbsp Garlic, fresh
  • 1 Jalapeno pepper, medium
  • 1 cup Red onion
  • 1 Tomato, medium
  • 3/4 tsp Sea salt
  • 1/4 cup Olive oil, extra-virgin
  • 2 tbsp Water
  • 1 tablespoon tikur azmud (see Tip), ground, or 1 teaspoon each ground cardamom and cumin

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat. Add chopped collards and cook until soft, about 15 minutes. Drain well in a colander.
  2. Add onion and 2 tablespoons water to the pot and cook over medium heat, stirring often, until the onion is translucent, 4 to 5 minutes. Add oil and garlic and cook, stirring, for 1 minute.
  3. Add tomato and jalape√±o; cook, stirring a few times, for 5 minutes.
  4. Add collards and stir, cover and cook, stirring once or twice, for 10 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low. Stir in tikur azmud (or cardamom and cumin), cover and cook for 3 minutes. Remove from heat. Stir in salt.
